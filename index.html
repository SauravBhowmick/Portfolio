Experience

Institute for Sustainable Energy Systems

Research Assistant

I served as a Master's Thesis Student on the Integrale Forschung Energie für Elektromobilität: Vom Modell zur Anwendung (iFEMA) project. My research focused on analyzing and predicting grid quality anomalies in power electronic industrial systems, employing cross-correlation analysis, curve fitting techniques, and GridVis software for effective anomaly detection. I successfully identified the most causally significant features contributing to these anomalies, which formed the foundation for developing machine learning models capable of forecasting grid quality issues. To address the inherent challenge of imbalanced data in anomaly detection, I implemented specialized imbalanced learning techniques, complementing these with neural network architectures designed for future voltage predictions. The technical stack for this work encompassed Python, Node-RED, InfluxDB, TensorFlow, Scikit-Learn, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn. Prior to my thesis work, I served as a Research Assistant on the same project from January to August 2024. In this role, I developed Python scripts that established reliable communication pathways between Beckhoff PLCs and MQTT servers, creating a robust foundation for industrial IoT applications. I automated critical data logging processes from both PLC systems and MQTT topics into MySQL databases, significantly enhancing data collection efficiency. My contributions to IoT-based data engineering solutions enabled real-time energy monitoring capabilities, leveraging technologies including Python, MQTT, MySQL, Beckhoff PLC, and Node-RED to create an integrated monitoring ecosystem.
